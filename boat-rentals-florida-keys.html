📍 Best Areas to Boat in the Florida Keys

Key Largo — John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park

The first underwater state park in the US. Rent a boat from Key Largo and you're minutes from some of the most spectacular reef snorkeling and diving in North America. The famous Christ of the Abyss statue and the Elbow reef are highlights.

Islamorada — The Backcountry

The "Sport Fishing Capital of the World." A maze of shallow mangrove channels, grass flats, and hidden coves that can only be reached by shallow-draft boat. Incredible bonefish, tarpon, and permit fishing. Also great for dolphin watching and eco-exploring.

Marathon — Seven Mile Bridge & Sandbar

Anchor under the famous Seven Mile Bridge or discover the sandbars and small uninhabited keys scattered across the Florida Bay. Marathon is centrally located — great base for exploring both upper and lower Keys by water.

Key West — Dry Tortugas & offshore

Key West is the southernmost point of the continental US and the gateway to the Dry Tortugas — a remote national park 70 miles west only accessible by boat or seaplane. Closer to Key West, the harbor, sandbars, and sunset cruise routes are iconic.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I rent a boat without a captain in the Florida Keys?

Yes, for smaller vessels. Florida requires boating safety certification for anyone born after January 1, 1988 operating a motorized vessel over 10HP. Many Keys operators offer bareboat rentals with a quick on-site orientation.

What's the best time of year to rent a boat in the Florida Keys?

The Keys have the best weather in Florida during November through May. Peak season is December through April. Summer is warm and humid — afternoon storms are common but mornings are usually calm and beautiful.

How far in advance should I book?

At least 1–2 weeks for regular dates. 4–6 weeks for spring break (March–April) and New Year's. Key West boats sell out fastest.
