Snorkeling in the Florida Keys 2026 — Reefs, Wrecks & Top Spots

Updated April 2026 Key Largo · Islamorada · Key West Reef From $35

The Florida Keys sit on top of the only living coral barrier reef in the continental United States. That single fact explains why snorkeling here is in a completely different league from anywhere else in the mainland. The reef runs 170 miles from Key Largo to the Dry Tortugas, and on a calm, clear day — which happens most of the year — visibility can reach 60–80 feet. You'll see coral formations, tropical fish, sea turtles, and sharks that look more like the Bahamas than Florida.

📍 Best Snorkeling Spots in the Florida Keys

🥇 Molasses Reef — Key Largo

Consistently ranked the best snorkeling site in the Keys and one of the top in North America. A large, diverse reef rising to within 6 feet of the surface with massive coral formations, abundant fish life, and excellent visibility. Accessible only by boat — most Key Largo tour operators make this their primary stop.

John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park — Key Largo

The first underwater state park in the US. In addition to the reef, the park is famous for the Christ of the Abyss — a 9-foot bronze statue submerged in 25 feet of water, visible to snorkelers looking down. Park concession boats run daily snorkel tours.

Cheeca Rocks — Islamorada

A shallow patch reef (3–15 feet deep) ideal for beginner snorkelers and families. Protected from currents, with colorful coral, sea fans, and abundant reef fish. One of the few sites accessible from shore with a short swim.

Looe Key Reef — Big Pine Key

A spectacular spur-and-groove reef system with visibility regularly exceeding 60 feet. Named for a British frigate that wrecked here in 1744. Marine life is extraordinarily diverse — sea turtles, nurse sharks, goliath grouper, and vibrant hard and soft coral.

Key West Reef

Multiple shallow reef sites within 5 miles of Key West. Sand Key, Rock Key, and Eastern Dry Rocks are the most popular — accessible on half-day snorkel charters that depart daily from the Key West Historic Seaport.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can you snorkel from shore in the Florida Keys?

A few spots — Cheeca Rocks in Islamorada and Bahia Honda State Park in Big Pine Key — offer accessible shore snorkeling. Most of the best reef sites require a boat trip of 3–8 miles offshore.

Are there sharks when snorkeling in the Florida Keys?

Nurse sharks are common and completely harmless — they lie motionless on the bottom and ignore snorkelers. Reef sharks are occasionally spotted but rarely approach people. The Keys have an excellent safety record for snorkeling.

What's the water temperature for snorkeling in the Keys?

Water temperature ranges from 72°F in winter to 86°F in summer. No wetsuit needed — a rashguard for sun protection is all most snorkelers wear.
