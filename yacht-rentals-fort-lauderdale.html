Fort Lauderdale earns its title as the "Yachting Capital of the World" every day. 🗺️ Best Yacht Routes in Fort Lauderdale

Las Olas Isles — "Millionaire's Row"

Cruise the Intracoastal through Las Olas Isles, where some of the most spectacular waterfront mansions in the US line the canals. Vessels ranging from 50-foot yachts to 300-foot superyachts are docked along this route — it's a floating showcase of American wealth and nautical engineering.

Port Everglades & Atlantic

Pass through Port Everglades — one of the busiest cruise ports in the world — and into the open Atlantic. On any given day you'll see massive cruise ships departing for the Caribbean. Head north along the beach to the Hillsboro Lighthouse or south toward the Miami skyline.

New River & Downtown Fort Lauderdale

The New River winds through the heart of downtown Fort Lauderdale past restaurants, the Riverwalk arts district, and Stranahan House — the oldest building in Broward County. An intimate and scenic route accessible only by boat.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Fort Lauderdale the Yachting Capital of the World?

165 miles of navigable waterways, 50,000+ registered yachts, the world's largest annual boat show, and more marinas per capita than anywhere else in the US. Fort Lauderdale is the global center of the superyacht industry, with hundreds of boats being built, refitted, and chartered here year-round.

Is Fort Lauderdale better than Miami for yachting?

Fort Lauderdale has more waterway mileage, more marinas, and arguably a more sophisticated charter scene. Miami has the more iconic skyline and bay. For a pure yachting experience, Fort Lauderdale is the more serious destination.
