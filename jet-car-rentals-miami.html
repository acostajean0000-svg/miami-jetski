💡 What is a Jet Car? Jet cars (also called Jetkar or water cars) are personal watercraft designed to look and feel like driving a convertible — but on water. They use the same jet propulsion as a jet ski but have a steering wheel, seats, and a car-like body. Speed tops out around 25–30 mph.

📍 Where to Ride a Jet Car in Miami

Most jet car operators launch from Miami Beach Marina or Biscayne Bay. The calm, sheltered bay waters are perfect for this type of watercraft. You can cruise past the famous "Millionaire's Row" of homes on Star Island and get up close to the Miami skyline — all from behind a steering wheel on the water.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a license to drive a jet car in Miami?

Florida's personal watercraft rules apply. If you were born after January 1, 1988, you'll need to complete a brief boating safety orientation — most operators provide this on-site at no extra charge.

How fast do jet cars go?

Jet cars typically reach 25–35 mph on open water. They're not designed for racing but for a fun, leisurely cruise with a unique aesthetic.

Is a jet car the same as a jet ski?

They use similar technology but look completely different. A jet car has a steering wheel, seats, and a car body. It's driven from a seated position like a car, not straddled like a jet ski.
