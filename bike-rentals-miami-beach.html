🗺️ Best Bike Routes in Miami Beach

The Beachfront Trail (South Beach)

A dedicated bike path runs parallel to the beach between 1st and 21st Street on the west side of Ocean Drive. Wide, paved, and mostly flat — perfect for beginners. Expect crowds on weekends; go early for a peaceful ride.

The Venetian Causeway

A scenic 2.6-mile causeway connecting Miami Beach to mainland Miami across a series of small islands. Beautiful water views in both directions and very little car traffic. One of Miami's hidden gems for cyclists.

Wynwood & Little Havana

Hop on a guided bike tour to explore the art and culture neighborhoods. Bobby's Bike Hike combines biking with stops at local food spots — an excellent way to eat your way through Miami.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Are there bike lanes in Miami Beach?

Yes. Miami Beach has dedicated bike lanes along many major roads and a car-free beachfront trail. The city has been expanding its cycling infrastructure significantly in recent years.

Can I ride a bike on the beach boardwalk in Miami?

Bikes are permitted on the beachfront path (west side of Ocean Drive) but not on the sand itself. The path is shared with pedestrians, so ride at a moderate speed and yield to walkers.

What is a Trikke?

A Trikke is a three-wheeled carving vehicle that you ride standing up, propelling yourself by shifting your weight. It's part cardio workout, part scooter, and wildly fun. South Florida Trikke Tours offers guided Trikke experiences along the Miami Beach boardwalk.
