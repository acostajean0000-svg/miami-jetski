Madeira Beach — Johns Pass and the Fishing Capital of Pinellas

Madeira Beach sits on a barrier island between Clearwater Beach to the north and St. Pete Beach to the south, centered on Johns Pass — a natural tidal inlet between Madeira Beach and Treasure Island. The pass creates an energetic current that concentrates fish and marine life, and Johns Pass Village has grown up around this natural focal point into one of the liveliest waterfront boardwalk communities on the Gulf Coast.

The centerpiece of Madeira Beach water sports is Hubbard's Marina — a Gulf Coast institution that has been running fishing and tour boats since the 1940s. The marina's party boat operation makes deep sea Gulf fishing accessible to anyone regardless of experience, while their touring fleet covers dolphin watching, shelling trips to Shell Key, and sightseeing around the barrier island chain. The combination of Johns Pass energy and Hubbard's breadth of operations makes Madeira Beach an outstanding water sports destination.

🎣 Hubbard's Marina — A Gulf Coast Legend

Hubbard's Marina was founded in the 1940s and has grown into one of the largest party boat fishing and tour operations in all of Florida. Their fleet includes multiple large party boats that can accommodate 40–100+ passengers, plus smaller tour vessels for dolphin watching and shelling. The deep sea party boat format is the most accessible and social way to experience Gulf of Mexico fishing — you share the boat with other anglers, all gear is provided, and the captains know exactly where the fish are on the nearshore and offshore ledges. Overnight grouper trips are a particular institution — anglers sleep on the boat and fish through the night on the 80–100 foot ledges.

Madeira Beach Water Sports Guide

Deep Sea Party Boat Fishing

Hubbard's Marina's party boat fishing is the signature Madeira Beach experience for anglers. The boats depart from Johns Pass and head into the Gulf of Mexico, reaching nearshore ledges in 40–60 feet of water for half-day trips, and the 80–100 foot offshore ledges for full-day and overnight excursions. Red snapper (when open), red grouper, gag grouper, lane snapper, cobia, and kingfish are the main targets. The party boat format means you're fishing alongside other passengers — a social, active experience that's great for groups or solo visitors who want to meet fellow anglers.

Dolphin Watching and Shelling Tours

For non-anglers, Hubbard's Marina tours provide outstanding access to the best of Pinellas County's coastal environment. Dolphin tours cruise through Johns Pass and out along the barrier island shoreline, where resident dolphin pods feed on the pass current and in the grass flats of Boca Ciega Bay. Shell Key shelling excursions take you to one of the best shelling destinations in the Tampa Bay area — an uninhabited island where lightning whelks, horse conchs, and sand dollars wash up on the Gulf-facing beach. The island is also a protected shorebird nesting site, making it excellent for birding.

Johns Pass Village

Beyond Hubbard's Marina, Johns Pass Village is worth exploring on foot. The boardwalk stretches along the pass with restaurants, seafood markets, souvenir shops, and multiple water sports rental operators for jet skis, parasailing, and kayaks. The pass itself is a great fishing spot — anglers line the bridge railings targeting snook, flounder, and Spanish mackerel, especially around the tide changes when baitfish get swept through the inlet.

Madeira Beach Water Sports FAQ

What is Hubbard's Marina and where is it?

Hubbard's Marina is located at 170 Boardwalk Place East, Madeira Beach FL 33708 — at Johns Pass Village on the south end of Madeira Beach. It's one of Florida's most famous fishing and boating operations, running deep sea fishing trips, dolphin tours, shelling excursions, and snorkeling trips since the 1940s. The marina is visible from the Johns Pass Bridge.

What fish can you catch on a Hubbard's Marina party boat?

Common catches include red grouper, gag grouper, red snapper (when the season is open), lane snapper, vermilion snapper, cobia, kingfish, Spanish mackerel, and amberjack. Seasons and regulations vary — the captains are fully licensed and knowledgeable about current regulations. Grouper is the signature Gulf of Mexico bottomfish and the primary target on most trips.

Is Johns Pass good for fishing?

Yes — Johns Pass is one of the better fishing spots in Pinellas County, particularly for snook, flounder, and Spanish mackerel. The pass current concentrates baitfish, and predator species stack up on the structure (bridge pilings, jetty rocks) waiting for easy meals. Anglers fish from the Johns Pass Bridge and adjacent jetties at no cost. The best action is typically during tidal movement — 2 hours before and after the tide change.

How far is Madeira Beach from Clearwater Beach?

Madeira Beach is approximately 10–12 miles south of Clearwater Beach along Gulf Boulevard — about 20–25 minutes by car. Both are part of the same Pinellas barrier island chain and are easily combined in a single day trip. Hubbard's Marina is the main attraction at Madeira Beach; Chadillac Adventures and the famous Pier 60 area are the highlights at Clearwater Beach.

What is Shell Key and can you shell there?

Shell Key is an uninhabited barrier island in Boca Ciega Bay, accessible from Madeira Beach by boat. It's one of the best shelling destinations in the Tampa Bay region — lightning whelks, horse conchs, fighting conchs, and sand dollars are commonly found on the Gulf-facing beach, especially at low tide. Hubbard's Marina runs shelling excursions to Shell Key; the island is also part of a protected preserve, so shells can be collected but must be empty (no live animals).
