🗺️ Best Party Routes in Fort Lauderdale

Intracoastal Waterway — Las Olas Isles

Cruise the "Millionaire's Row" of Fort Lauderdale — channel after channel of spectacular waterfront mansions, mega yachts at private docks, and the beautiful Las Olas drawbridge. Pull up to a waterfront restaurant for drinks, then cruise on. This is the quintessential Fort Lauderdale experience.

Hollywood Beach Sandbar

Anchor at the shallow sandbar off Hollywood Beach for swimming, music, and socializing. On weekends, dozens of boats cluster here for a floating party scene. Easy access for both large charter boats and BYOB pontoons.

Port Everglades & Atlantic

Head out through the port for open Atlantic water — more room to move, bigger waves to bounce on, and the dramatic backdrop of cruise ships coming and going. Good for groups that want a more adventurous, open-water experience.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most popular party boat experience in Fort Lauderdale?

The BYOB pontoon rental on the Intracoastal is the crowd favorite — affordable, completely private for your group, and uniquely Fort Lauderdale. Baymingo's social cruises are the top pick for solo travelers and small groups wanting to meet new people.

How do I split the cost of a private party boat rental?

Most private pontoons hold 8–12 people. At $400–$650 total, that works out to $40–$80 per person — comparable to a bar tab, with the whole waterway as your venue.
