🐟 What to Target in Key West

Offshore — The Gulf Stream

The Gulf Stream passes close to Key West, bringing mahi-mahi, sailfish, wahoo, and bluefin tuna within reach. A half-day offshore run (15–20 miles) puts you on blue water quickly. Peak season: March–July for mahi, November–February for sailfish.

Backcountry — Florida Bay Flats

A vast expanse of shallow grass flats extending north of Key West into Florida Bay. The holy trinity of flats fishing — bonefish, permit, and tarpon — all live here. Sight-fishing from a shallow skiff to tailing fish is the pinnacle of the sport. This is what draws serious anglers from around the world to Key West.

Nearshore Reefs & Wrecks

The reef system along the Atlantic side of the Keys is loaded with mangrove snapper, yellowtail snapper, grouper, and amberjack. Half-day reef trips are the most affordable option and deliver plenty of action for families and beginners.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a fishing license in Key West?

No. The captain's license covers all passengers when aboard a licensed charter. You do not need a personal saltwater fishing license.

What should I bring on a Key West fishing charter?

Sunscreen (reef-safe preferred), polarized sunglasses, a hat, light breathable clothing, non-slip shoes, snacks, and water. All fishing equipment is provided.

Is Key West good for beginner anglers?

Absolutely. Reef and party boat trips are beginner-friendly with the mate handling most of the technical work. Backcountry and offshore trips can be more challenging but guides are patient and instructional.
