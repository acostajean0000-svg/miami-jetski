Water Sports on Anna Maria Island

Anna Maria Island is a 7-mile barrier island at the northern end of Sarasota Bay, where Tampa Bay meets the Gulf of Mexico. It's one of the last places on Florida's Gulf Coast that still feels genuinely "Old Florida" — no high-rises, no chain hotels, low-key local character. The water around the island reflects this personality: clear, calm, and teeming with wildlife.

The island has two distinct water environments. The Gulf side offers open water, white sand beaches, and a light surf — ideal for jet skiing, parasailing, and swimming. The bay side — facing Tampa Bay and Sarasota Bay — is protected, shallow, and home to one of the most productive wildlife ecosystems on the Gulf Coast. Manatees winter in the warm, shallow bay waters. Dolphins patrol the grass flats. Ospreys and roseate spoonbills nest in the mangroves of nearby Emerson Point.

🦭 Manatees at Anna Maria Island

West Indian manatees are commonly seen in the warm, shallow waters of Tampa Bay and Sarasota Bay around Anna Maria Island, especially from November through March when they seek warm water near power plant discharge areas and natural springs. Kayaking the bay side gives you the best chance of a manatee encounter — these gentle giants often surface just feet from paddlers.

Anna Maria Island Water Activities Guide

Kayaking & Paddleboarding the Bay

The bay side of Anna Maria Island — facing Tampa Bay — is one of the best flatwater kayaking environments on the Gulf Coast. The Yak Shack's mangrove tours at Emerson Point take paddlers through narrow mangrove tunnels where roots arch overhead and wildlife crowds the water's edge. Self-guided rentals let you explore at your own pace. Early morning paddles are magical as the mist lifts off the water and ospreys begin fishing.

Fishing in Tampa Bay and the Gulf

Anna Maria Island sits at the confluence of Tampa Bay and Sarasota Bay — two of Florida's most productive inshore fisheries. Snook, redfish, sea trout, and tarpon patrol the grass flats and tidal passes. Father Daughter Charters and Robin Hood specialize in making fishing accessible for all ages and experience levels. Offshore, the Gulf produces Spanish mackerel, grouper, and amberjack on the nearshore ledges.

Sunset Cruises & Boat Tours

Anna Maria Island's west-facing Gulf coast produces legendary sunsets — the sky turns vivid orange and purple over the water as the sun drops. Sunset cruises departing from the island's north end marinas are one of the most popular evening activities. Some charters combine a dolphin watch with the sunset for a two-in-one experience.

Anna Maria Island Water Sports FAQ

What water sports are available on Anna Maria Island?

Anna Maria Island offers boat charters and fishing tours, kayaking and paddleboarding in Sarasota Bay and Tampa Bay, jet skiing on the Gulf, dolphin watching tours, parasailing, snorkeling, and sunset cruises.

Is Anna Maria Island good for kayaking?

Yes — the bay side offers flat, protected water with abundant wildlife including dolphins, manatees, ospreys, and sea turtles. Yak Shack operates mangrove tour kayak adventures from nearby Emerson Point, showcasing some of the best paddling on Florida's Gulf Coast.

Can you see dolphins at Anna Maria Island?

Yes — bottlenose dolphins are abundant around Anna Maria Island year-round. They're commonly spotted on kayak tours in the bay, on fishing charters near the passes, and from the shoreline.

What is the best time to visit Anna Maria Island for water sports?

December through April is ideal — warm, sunny, low humidity, and calm Gulf waters. Morning sessions year-round are recommended for the calmest conditions before afternoon sea breezes build.

How far is Anna Maria Island from Sarasota?

Anna Maria Island is about 20 miles north of downtown Sarasota — roughly a 30–40 minute drive. It's also about 50 miles south of Tampa (1 hour).
