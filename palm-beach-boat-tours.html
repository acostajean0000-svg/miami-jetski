Palm Beach — America's Most Spectacular Waterfront

Palm Beach Island is one of the wealthiest communities on earth, and the most dramatic way to experience it is from the water. The west-facing shoreline of Palm Beach Island looks across Lake Worth Lagoon toward West Palm Beach — a broad, calm Intracoastal Waterway channel lined on the island side with some of the most extraordinary private estates in America. The "Millionaire's Row" boat tour is a Palm Beach institution, giving visitors access to a perspective impossible from land.

Beyond the estate tour circuit, Palm Beach County's waterways offer an impressive range of boat tour experiences — from the reef snorkel runs off Palm Beach Inlet to the wildlife-rich Jupiter Inlet tours in the north. The Gulf Stream runs close to shore here, keeping water temperatures moderate and supporting a rich marine ecosystem year-round.

🏛️ Millionaire's Row — Estate Tour Highlights

The Palm Beach Millionaire's Row boat tour passes estates with extraordinary histories. The waterfront has been home to the Rockefellers, the Kennedys (the Kennedy Compound), Donald Trump's Mar-a-Lago, and scores of other celebrated names. Many properties date to the early 20th century Gilded Age development of Palm Beach by Henry Flagler, who connected the island to the mainland by rail and built its first grand hotels. The story of Palm Beach's development is inseparable from American economic history.

Palm Beach Boat Tours by Activity

Millionaire's Row & Lake Worth Lagoon

The signature Palm Beach boat experience is the Millionaire's Row tour on Lake Worth Lagoon. Departing from the West Palm Beach waterfront, boats cross to the Palm Beach Island side and travel south past the extraordinary estate waterfront. The channel is wide, calm, and well-protected — smooth water in nearly any conditions. Visit Palm Beach runs multiple daily tours with knowledgeable guides who provide estate histories and Palm Beach social commentary. Sunset cruises on this same route are spectacular as the estates silhouette against the evening sky.

Reef Snorkel Boat Tours

The Palm Beach Inlet reef sits just offshore from the island, accessible by boat in under 15 minutes from West Palm Beach. Visit Palm Beach's snorkel tours combine a brief transit with substantial time on the reef — usually 45–60 minutes of actual snorkeling. The reef hosts a remarkable concentration of sea turtles (loggerhead and green), which are particularly common near the inlet due to the natural current that carries jellyfish and other prey. Tropical fish populations are excellent — angelfish, parrotfish, sergeant majors, and dozens of other species.

Jupiter Inlet Dolphin & Wildlife Tours

Pontiki's Jupiter Inlet tours offer a completely different Palm Beach County boat experience — nature-focused rather than estate-focused. The Jupiter Inlet is one of the most active dolphin habitats in South Florida, with resident pods that feed in the inlet current year-round. The tour also covers the beautiful Loxahatchee River estuary with its scenic mangroves and the historic Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse providing a constant landmark. Manatee sightings are common in the quieter back-bay sections.

Palm Beach Boat Tours FAQ

What boat tours are available in Palm Beach?

Palm Beach offers Millionaire's Row estate tours on Lake Worth Lagoon, sunset cruises, reef snorkel boat tours to the Palm Beach Inlet, dolphin watching in Jupiter Inlet, private charters for fishing and custom routes, and the unique night shark drift experience with Xela Expeditions. Tours cover the full length of Palm Beach County's waterways.

What is Millionaire's Row in Palm Beach?

Millionaire's Row is the stretch of Palm Beach Island waterfront on Lake Worth Lagoon, lined with extraordinary private estates — some of the most valuable residential properties in the United States. The estates include Mar-a-Lago, the former Kennedy family compound, and dozens of Gilded Age mansions built by Henry Flagler's era railroad elite. Boat tours give access to this perspective that's simply unavailable from land.

How long are Palm Beach boat tours?

Standard tours run 2–3 hours. Millionaire's Row and sunset cruises are typically 2 hours. Reef snorkel tours run 3–4 hours including reef time. Private charters can be arranged from 2 to 8 hours with customizable itineraries. Jupiter Inlet tours with Pontiki are approximately 2 hours.

Can you see sea turtles on Palm Beach boat tours?

Yes — sea turtles are commonly sighted on the reef snorkel tours near Palm Beach Inlet, and occasionally spotted during Lake Worth Lagoon tours as well. Palm Beach County has one of the most active sea turtle nesting beaches in the United States; loggerheads and leatherbacks both nest on Palm Beach Island beaches May through October. The reef at Palm Beach Inlet is a reliable feeding ground for green and loggerhead turtles year-round.

Is Palm Beach good for sunset boat tours?

Palm Beach is exceptional for sunset cruises. Lake Worth Lagoon faces west, meaning the sunset light falls directly on the water and the Palm Beach Island estate skyline. The warm Gulf Stream-influenced Atlantic air creates spectacular evening colors November through April. Visit Palm Beach runs dedicated sunset cruise departures timed to the golden hour, and the calm lagoon water makes for smooth sailing in virtually any conditions.
