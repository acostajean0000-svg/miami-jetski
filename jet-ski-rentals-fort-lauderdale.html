📍 Best Jet Ski Spots in Fort Lauderdale

Hollywood Beach

One of South Florida's most popular beaches, Hollywood sits between Fort Lauderdale and Miami. The water inside the sandbar is calm and perfect for beginners. The famous 2.5-mile Broadwalk runs parallel to the beach — anchor up and walk for a bite to eat. Several operators launch directly from the beach here.

Intracoastal Waterway

Fort Lauderdale's extensive canal system connects to the Intracoastal Waterway, giving jet skiers a unique urban experience — cruising past luxury yachts, waterfront mansions, and the downtown bridges. Stay in marked channels and watch for boat traffic.

Pompano Beach

Just north of Fort Lauderdale, Pompano offers a more laid-back vibe with excellent open-water access. The beach itself is less crowded than Hollywood, and you can ride north toward Hillsboro Inlet or south toward Fort Lauderdale Beach. The pier area makes a great landmark to cruise around.

Dania Beach

South of Fort Lauderdale, Dania Beach is a hidden gem for water sports — less crowded, easy parking, and direct access to both the Intracoastal and the Atlantic through the Dania Beach inlet.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I ride a jet ski on the Intracoastal in Fort Lauderdale?

Yes, but you must stay in marked channels, maintain no-wake speed near docks and bridges, and watch carefully for boat traffic. The ICW is busy with large vessels — experienced riders only in high-traffic sections.

Is Fort Lauderdale jet skiing better than Miami?

Different, not better. Fort Lauderdale has the unique Intracoastal experience and Hollywood Beach's calm inner waters. Miami has Biscayne Bay's skyline backdrop and Star Island. Both are excellent — it depends on what kind of scenery you prefer.

What age do you need to be to rent a jet ski in Fort Lauderdale?

Riders must be at least 16. Under 18 requires a parent or guardian signature. Florida requires a boating safety orientation for anyone born after January 1, 1988 — most operators provide this on-site.
