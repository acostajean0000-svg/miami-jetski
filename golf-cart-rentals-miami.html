🏘️ Best Neighborhoods to Explore by Golf Cart

Wynwood

Miami's world-famous arts district is perfect for golf carts — narrow streets, no through traffic, and a mural on every corner. Explore at your own pace and stop wherever you like for photos, food, or a cold drink.

South Beach / Art Deco District

The pastel-colored Art Deco buildings of Ocean Drive are best appreciated slowly. A golf cart lets you cruise Collins and Washington Avenues, stopping at any of the historic hotels for a quick look inside.

Little Havana

Calle Ocho is one of Miami's most culturally rich streets. Ride through and stop at a Cuban café, a cigar shop, or the famous Máximo Gómez Park where locals play dominoes every day.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a driver's license to rent a golf cart in Miami?

Yes. A valid driver's license is required to rent and operate a street-legal golf cart in Miami. The minimum age is typically 21.

Can golf carts drive on Miami streets?

Street-legal golf carts (Low Speed Vehicles / LSVs) can drive on roads with speed limits of 35 mph or less in Florida. Most Miami neighborhood streets qualify.
