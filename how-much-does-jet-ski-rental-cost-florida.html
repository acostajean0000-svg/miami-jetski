How Much Does a Jet Ski Rental Cost in Florida? — 2026

Updated April 2026 5 min read All Florida

Jet ski rental prices in Florida vary more than most people expect — from as low as $89/hour to over $300 depending on where you are, who you book with, and whether it's a guided tour or a self-guided rental. This guide breaks down the real 2026 numbers based on our database of 31 jet ski operators across Florida.

Short answer: Expect to pay $89–$160 per hour for a standard jet ski rental in most of Florida. Miami Beach tends to be on the higher end ($120–$200), while Gulf Coast cities like Naples and Fort Myers average $89–$150.

What's Included in the Price?

Most jet ski rentals in Florida include:

  • Life jacket (required by Florida law)
  • Brief safety orientation (usually 10–15 min)
  • Fuel
  • Basic insurance

What's usually not included:

  • Damage deposits ($200–$500, refundable)
  • Upgraded insurance / damage waiver (add $20–$50)
  • GoPro or photo packages (add $30–$60)
  • Fuel surcharge on some longer rentals

Tips to Get the Best Price

  • Book on weekdays: Weekend prices can be 15–25% higher at popular spots.
  • Book in advance: Last-minute bookings often cost more, especially in peak season (Dec–Mar).
  • Go 2+ hours: The per-hour rate almost always drops significantly for multi-hour rentals.
  • Bring your own group: One jet ski for 2 riders is nearly the same price as 1 rider — split the cost.
  • Avoid holiday weekends: Memorial Day, July 4th, and Labor Day are the most expensive days of the year.
  • Compare before you book: Prices for the same duration can vary by $50–$80 between operators in the same city.

Self-Guided vs. Guided Tour — Which is Cheaper?

Guided tours typically cost $99–$209 and include a guide who leads a set route, often past local landmarks. Self-guided rentals give you full freedom but require more experience. For a first-timer, a guided tour can actually be better value — you get a built-in route and safety instruction included in the price.

FAQs About Jet Ski Rental Costs

Is the price per jet ski or per person?

Almost always per jet ski — not per person. A jet ski that fits 2–3 riders costs the same as one with a solo rider. This makes it great value for couples and small groups.

Are there hidden fees?

The most common "hidden" fee is the damage deposit ($200–$500), which is fully refunded if there are no issues. Some operators also charge a fuel surcharge for longer rentals or premium jet ski models.

Can I negotiate the price?

Most operators have fixed rates, but calling directly (rather than booking through a third party) sometimes yields a small discount, especially for longer durations or groups booking multiple jet skis.
