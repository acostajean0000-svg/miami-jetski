Palm Beach County — From the Lagoon to the Gulf Stream

Palm Beach County stretches across 47 miles of Atlantic coastline, from the fashionable Intracoastal Waterways of West Palm Beach and Palm Beach Island in the south to the pristine Jupiter Inlet and Loxahatchee River estuary in the north. This geographic range creates a remarkable variety of water sports environments — calm lagoon paddling, open reef snorkeling, shark dive adventures, fishing on the Gulf Stream edge, and scenic boat tours past some of America's most valuable real estate.

The waters here are defined by Lake Worth Lagoon — the wide Intracoastal Waterway stretch between Palm Beach Island and the mainland — and the Palm Beach Inlet, which connects the lagoon to the Atlantic and creates a rich reef environment right at the beach. To the north, Jupiter Inlet and the Loxahatchee River form one of the most scenic and wildlife-rich paddling corridors in South Florida.

Xela Expeditions' night shark drift is one of the most unique marine experiences in all of Florida. West Palm Beach Water Sports Guide

Lake Worth Lagoon — The Heart of West Palm Water Life

Lake Worth Lagoon is the broad Intracoastal Waterway channel that separates the city of West Palm Beach from Palm Beach Island. It's the focal point for most boat-based activities in the area — the wide, calm water is ideal for tours, sunset cruises, and paddling. Visit Palm Beach runs guided tours that cover Millionaire's Row, the historic Palm Beach Biltmore area, and the worth Avenue waterfront with its incredible concentration of waterfront estates.

Palm Beach Inlet Reef — Snorkeling & Diving

The Palm Beach Inlet creates a natural current that sweeps nutrients and marine life past the adjacent reef system, making it one of the richest reef snorkeling sites in South Florida. Sea turtles are common — loggerheads and greens both use the inlet corridor. The reef is in 10–25 feet of water and features healthy coral formations, moray eels, nurse sharks, tropical reef fish, and in season, Atlantic spotted dolphins. Visit Palm Beach's snorkel tours make this accessible without requiring diving certification.

Jupiter Inlet Kayaking

The Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse Outstanding Natural Area is the departure point for Get Up & Go Kayaking's tours — one of the most popular paddling experiences in Palm Beach County. The route covers the Loxahatchee River estuary — Florida's only National Wild and Scenic River — with its ancient cypress trees, clear spring water, and abundant wildlife. Dolphins, manatees, river otters, and dozens of bird species are regularly spotted. The historic Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse (1860) provides a stunning backdrop throughout the paddle.

West Palm Beach Water Sports FAQ

What water sports are available in West Palm Beach?

West Palm Beach and Palm Beach County offer boat tours on Lake Worth Lagoon, reef snorkeling at Palm Beach Inlet, night shark drift tours, kayaking Jupiter Inlet, fishing charters, paddleboarding, parasailing, and sunset cruises. The Gulf Stream runs just 3–4 miles offshore, creating excellent conditions for both reef and blue-water activities.

Can you snorkel in West Palm Beach?

Yes — the Palm Beach Inlet reef is an excellent snorkel site with sea turtles, tropical fish, and nurse sharks in 10–25 feet of clear Atlantic water. For a more adventurous experience, Xela Expeditions offers night shark drift snorkeling where you drift through a natural aggregation of sharks after dark. Both options are guided by expert local operators.

Is the night shark dive in Palm Beach safe?

Xela Expeditions has an excellent safety record with their night shark drift experiences. The sharks (primarily nurse and lemon) are wild but accustomed to the site, and guides are in the water throughout. The experience is designed for confident swimmers rather than novices — participants must be comfortable in open water at night. It is not a cage dive; participants are free-swimming in the water with the sharks.

Is Jupiter worth visiting for water sports?

Absolutely — Jupiter is one of the best water sports destinations in Palm Beach County. The Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse area offers some of the finest guided kayaking in South Florida, with the scenic Loxahatchee River, abundant wildlife, and the beautiful Intracoastal channels. The Jupiter area also has excellent reef diving, fishing, and the Blue Heron Bridge in nearby Riviera Beach is considered one of the best shore diving sites in the world.

When is the best time for water sports in West Palm Beach?

November through April offers the best overall conditions — calm seas, excellent water clarity, pleasant air temperatures (72–82°F), and peak wildlife activity including manatees, sea turtles, and migratory birds. Summer offers warm water and good snorkeling but afternoon thunderstorms are a regular feature June through September, often cutting afternoon activities short.
