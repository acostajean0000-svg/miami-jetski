St. Petersburg & Tampa Bay — Florida's Greatest Estuary

St. Petersburg occupies the tip of the Pinellas Peninsula, surrounded on three sides by Tampa Bay — one of the largest and most productive estuaries in the southeastern United States. The city has reinvented itself into a vibrant waterfront destination, with the St. Pete Pier, downtown waterfront parks, and the iconic Tampa Riverwalk (across the bay) all accessible by water. St. Pete Beach, on the barrier island to the west, connects to the Gulf of Mexico and the Pinellas barrier island chain.

The combination of Tampa Bay's protected, productive waters and the Gulf of Mexico's open-water opportunities makes the St. Pete area one of the most water-sport-rich destinations in Florida. Boat rentals on Tampa Bay open access to incredible inshore fishing, Shell Key shelling excursions, Fort De Soto Park, and some of the finest dolphin-watching waters anywhere. To the north, the barrier islands of Clearwater and Madeira Beach extend the range further.

🌉 The Sunshine Skyway Bridge

The Bob Graham Sunshine Skyway Bridge spans Tampa Bay at its mouth — a 4.1-mile cable-stayed bridge soaring 430 feet above the water at its highest point. The fishing piers below (on the remnants of the old bridge) are the most productive pier fishing sites in Florida, targeting kingfish, cobia, tarpon, and Spanish mackerel. The bridge is also a stunning landmark visible from virtually any point on Tampa Bay — and spectacular from a helicopter tour.

St. Pete / Tampa Bay Water Sports Guide

Boat Rentals on Tampa Bay

Tampa Bay is one of the most rewarding boat rental destinations in Florida — 400 square miles of sheltered estuary with hundreds of miles of shoreline, dozens of islands, and exceptional wildlife. TV Boat Rentals in St. Petersburg puts you at the center of this world, with quick access to Shell Key (excellent shelling, dolphins, birds), Fort De Soto (7 miles of pristine beach and a historic fort), and the open bay fishing grounds. The bay's shallow, protected water makes it ideal for first-time or casual boaters.

Tampa Riverwalk — Urban Water Adventure

The Tampa Riverwalk offers a completely different kind of water experience. Kayaking or paddleboarding the Hillsborough River through downtown Tampa puts you at water level looking up at Tampa's dramatic skyline, the iconic Tampa Theatre, the University of Tampa (housed in the ornate Henry B. Plant Hotel from 1891), and the bridges and walkways of the Riverwalk itself. Tampa Riverwalk Rentals makes this accessible and easy — rentals by the hour, no experience required.

Helicopter Tours — Tampa Bay from Above

The aerial tour over Tampa Bay is a bucket-list experience for water sports enthusiasts who want a different kind of perspective. Routes typically cover the downtown Tampa waterfront, the Hillsborough Bay, the expanse of Old Tampa Bay, the Sunshine Skyway Bridge from 430 feet above the deck level, and the barrier island chain stretching north toward Clearwater. At $55–$150 per person depending on route length, it's one of the best value aerial experiences in the state.

Tampa Bay Fishing — The Redfish Capital

Tampa Bay is world-famous in fishing circles for its exceptional shallow-water redfishing. The grass flats and oyster bars of the bay support one of the strongest redfish populations in the US, and local guides specialize in sight-fishing for these copper-colored bruisers on the skinny water. Spring and fall are peak seasons — May and October in particular can produce extraordinary days. Snook follow the mangrove shorelines, and the tarpon run through the passes (especially Egmont Key pass at the bay mouth) is a May–July spectacle.

St. Pete Beach Water Sports FAQ

What water sports are available in St. Petersburg Florida?

St. Petersburg offers boat rentals on Tampa Bay, kayaking and paddleboarding on the Riverwalk, helicopter aerial tours, jet ski rentals, parasailing, dolphin watching cruises, fishing charters (inshore and offshore), and sunset cruises. The Tampa Bay estuary provides exceptional protected-water activities, while the Gulf of Mexico is accessible via the Pinellas barrier islands to the west.

Can you rent a boat in St. Pete Beach?

Yes — TV Boat Rentals is based in St. Petersburg on the Pinellas Bayway (near the St. Pete Beach causeway), offering boat rentals for exploring Tampa Bay, Boca Ciega Bay, and the surrounding waterways. Their boats provide access to Shell Key, Fort De Soto, the Sunshine Skyway area, and the productive fishing grounds of the bay.

What is Fort De Soto and how do you get there?

Fort De Soto County Park is one of the finest parks in the entire country — 1,136 acres on five interconnected islands at the mouth of Tampa Bay. It has 7 miles of white sand beach, exceptional shelling, a kayak/canoe trail through mangroves, bird sanctuaries, and the ruins of a Spanish-American War-era military fort. It's accessible by car (Pinellas Bayway toll) or by boat from St. Pete Beach. The park's beaches are stunning and far less crowded than Clearwater Beach.

Is the Tampa Riverwalk good for kayaking?

Yes — the Tampa Riverwalk is a unique and enjoyable kayaking experience. Paddling the Hillsborough River through downtown Tampa gives a water-level perspective on the city's remarkable architecture and waterfront. Tampa Riverwalk Rentals makes it easy with hourly kayak and paddleboard rentals right on the promenade. Early morning is ideal — calm water, soft light, and the city waking up around you.

How far is St. Pete Beach from Clearwater Beach?

St. Pete Beach is approximately 20–25 miles south of Clearwater Beach along the Pinellas barrier island chain — about 30–45 minutes by car via Gulf Boulevard (Route 699). By boat, the two are connected through the Intracoastal Waterway, with beautiful barrier island scenery the entire way. Many visitors do both in a single day trip.
