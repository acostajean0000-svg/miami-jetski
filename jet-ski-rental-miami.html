📋 What You Need to Know Before You Go

Florida's warm weather and calm intracoastal waters make jet skiing accessible to almost anyone. Here are the key rules and tips to make your rental experience smooth:

🪪
No License Required
Florida law allows anyone 14+ to operate a PWC without a boating license after completing a brief on-site safety orientation provided by the operator. A valid photo ID is required at check-in.
🎂
Age Requirements
Renters must be 18+ to book. Riders aged 14–17 can operate with a signed adult waiver. Children under 14 can ride as a passenger with a licensed adult at the controls.
👥
2 Riders Per Ski
All Miami rental jet skis are 2-person capable (Sea-Doo Spark 2-Up or Yamaha WaveRunner EX Sport). No extra charge for a second rider in most cases — confirm at booking.
⛽
Fuel Included
All rental prices include fuel. You'll never pay a gas surcharge. Life jackets, kill-switch lanyards, and basic safety gear are also always provided at no extra cost.
👙
What to Wear
Wear a swimsuit or athletic wear. Leave valuables on shore or in a waterproof bag. Most operators have free lockers. Secure sunglasses with a strap — you will get wet.
🌤️
Best Time to Go
November through April offers the most comfortable temperatures (75–82°F) and calmer waters. Weekday mornings before 10am are the quietest. Summer is hot but the water is warm and clear.

Florida PWC Speed Rules

All personal watercraft in Florida must observe a idle-speed / no-wake zone within 300 feet of shorelines, docks, and designated swim areas. Open-water speeds are unrestricted in marked navigation channels. Your operator will brief you on the specific rules for your launch zone before you depart.

Riding under the influence of alcohol is a federal and state offense. Reckless operation can result in a fine of up to $1,000 and loss of your booking deposit. All operators enforce strict safety policies.
