Where to Rent a Jet Ski in Miami Beach

Miami Beach has several popular launch points, each with its own vibe:

  • South Beach (1st–20th St): The most iconic spot. Busy but scenic — you'll ride past the Art Deco district and the famous SoBe skyline. Great for photos.
  • Mid-Beach / Bal Harbour: Less crowded than South Beach, with cleaner water and a more relaxed atmosphere. Ideal for beginners.
  • North Miami Beach / Haulover: A local favorite. You can access open ocean with fewer restrictions and more space to open up the throttle.
  • Coconut Grove / Dinner Key Marina: Beautiful Biscayne Bay views. A bit further from the beach crowd, perfect for a scenic bay tour on a jet ski.

What to Know Before You Ride

Do you need a license?

In Florida, anyone born after January 1, 1988 must carry a valid Boating Safety Education ID Card to operate a personal watercraft. Most rental operators offer a quick on-site safety course (10–15 minutes) that satisfies this requirement. Bring a valid ID.

Age requirements

The minimum age to drive a jet ski in Florida is 14 years old (with an adult on board) or 18 years old to ride solo. Rules vary by operator — always check before booking if you're bringing younger riders.

What to bring

  • Valid government-issued ID
  • Sunscreen (apply before you arrive — you'll get wet)
  • Water shoes or sandals with heel straps
  • A waterproof case for your phone
  • Cash or credit card for any security deposit

Best Time to Rent a Jet Ski in Miami Beach

Year-round: Miami's climate is ideal for jet skiing 365 days a year, with water temperatures rarely dropping below 72°F. That said, some seasons are better than others:

  • October–April: Peak season. Dry, sunny weather, lower humidity. Book in advance — especially December through February when snowbirds fill the area.
  • May–September: Summer. Hot and humid with occasional afternoon thunderstorms. Morning rides (before noon) are your best bet to avoid weather and crowds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bring a passenger on the jet ski?

Yes — most jet skis accommodate 2–3 riders. The price is usually the same per jet ski regardless of number of riders, making it great value for couples or small groups.

Do I need to know how to swim?

Knowing how to swim is highly recommended, though operators will provide a life jacket (required by Florida law). The water in Biscayne Bay is relatively calm and shallow in most rental areas.

Can I go into the open ocean?

This depends on the operator and conditions. Many restrict riders to Biscayne Bay or designated zones. Some operators, particularly in North Miami Beach and Haulover, allow ocean access. Ask when booking if ocean access is important to you.
