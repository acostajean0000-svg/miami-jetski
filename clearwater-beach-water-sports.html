Clearwater Beach — Florida's Gulf Coast at Its Best

Clearwater Beach sits on a barrier island at the southern end of Pinellas County, separated from the mainland by Clearwater Harbor and connected by the Memorial Causeway. It has won TripAdvisor's Best Beach in the United States award multiple times, and the combination of fine white quartz sand, calm emerald Gulf water, and excellent water sports infrastructure makes it easy to see why.

The water here is defined by two distinct environments. On the Gulf side — the west-facing beach — the open Gulf of Mexico offers warm, relatively calm water with good visibility. On the harbor side, Clearwater Harbor is a protected, shallow-water estuary ringed by mangroves and grass flats, teeming with dolphins, manatees, sea turtles, and the full suite of inshore fish species. Boat rentals give access to both worlds, plus the incredible cluster of undeveloped barrier islands to the north — Caladesi Island and Honeymoon Island — that are among the finest natural beach destinations anywhere in Florida.

🐬 Clearwater Harbor Dolphins

Clearwater Harbor has one of the most well-studied bottlenose dolphin populations in the world, thanks to decades of research by the Clearwater Marine Aquarium (the home of Winter the dolphin, star of Dolphin Tale). Resident pods totaling 100+ individuals use the harbor year-round, feeding on the rich grass flat fish populations. Dolphin encounters on any boat trip in the harbor are essentially guaranteed — the animals are accustomed to boats and often approach for a closer look.

Islands Accessible by Boat from Clearwater

🏝️ Caladesi Island

One of Florida's last undeveloped barrier islands. 3 miles of pristine Gulf beach, mangrove kayak trail, exceptional shelling. Boat or ferry access only.

🌴 Honeymoon Island

State park connected to mainland, with ferry to Caladesi. Nature trails, osprey nests, Gulf swimming, and the ferry departure point.

🐚 Shell Key

Uninhabited island in Boca Ciega Bay famous for shelling. Popular day-trip destination from Madeira Beach and St. Pete Beach.

🐠 Sand Key

Sand Key County Park — just south of Clearwater Beach pass. Quieter beach, great snorkeling at the jetties, dolphins in the pass.

🦅 Three Rooker Island

Uninhabited state-owned island accessible by boat. Excellent birding, shelling, and secluded beach time. No facilities.

🎣 Anclote Keys

Lighthouse island at the northern edge of Pinellas County. Remote, beautiful, excellent for fishing and wildlife. Day trip by boat from Clearwater.

Clearwater Beach Water Sports Guide

Boat Rentals — Explore the Harbor Chain

A boat rental from Chadillac Adventures unlocks the full Clearwater barrier island chain. The standard half-day run covers Clearwater Harbor (dolphins, grass flats, mangrove channels), then north through the gap between Caladesi and Honeymoon Islands to anchor on Caladesi's Gulf beach — one of the finest stretches of undeveloped coast anywhere in Florida. Full-day rentals extend the range to Anclote Keys to the north or Shell Key to the south, making for an extraordinary day of coastal exploration.

Deep Sea Fishing at Hubbard's Marina

Hubbard's Marina in neighboring Madeira Beach is a Pinellas County institution that has been running fishing trips for over 70 years. Their party boat operation is the most accessible way to experience Gulf of Mexico fishing — no charter required, all equipment provided, and the boats target grouper, snapper, cobia, and kingfish on the nearshore and offshore ledges. Full-day trips reach the 60–100 foot ledges where the best grouper fishing occurs. The marina also runs excellent dolphin-watching and shelling tours for non-anglers.

Kayaking and Paddleboarding

Chadillac Adventures' Cove Cay location offers a more intimate water experience — kayak and paddleboard rentals in a protected cove setting with access to Clearwater's inland waterway network. The mangrove channels of Clearwater Harbor are outstanding for kayaking, with clear shallow water, abundant wildlife, and the dramatic skyline of Clearwater Beach in the background. Early morning (7–9 a.m.) is the best time for calm water and maximum wildlife activity.

Clearwater Beach Water Sports FAQ

What water sports are available at Clearwater Beach?

Clearwater Beach offers boat rentals, dolphin watching tours, parasailing, jet skiing, kayaking, paddleboarding, deep sea fishing (at Hubbard's Marina in nearby Madeira Beach), snorkeling trips, sunset cruises, and Caladesi Island excursions. The combination of calm harbor water and open Gulf access makes it one of the most versatile water sports destinations on the Gulf Coast.

Can you rent a boat at Clearwater Beach?

Yes — Chadillac Adventures offers boat rentals directly from Clearwater Beach. Boats are available for half-day and full-day rentals, giving access to Clearwater Harbor, the Gulf of Mexico, Caladesi Island, Honeymoon Island, and the surrounding waterways. No boating license is required in Florida for vessels under 26 feet for those born before 1988.

Is Clearwater Beach good for dolphin watching?

Clearwater Beach is one of the best dolphin watching destinations in all of Florida. Clearwater Harbor has a large resident bottlenose dolphin population (studied by the Clearwater Marine Aquarium for decades), and dolphin encounters on any harbor boat trip are essentially guaranteed. The dolphins are habituated to boats and frequently approach vessels out of curiosity.

What is Caladesi Island and can you visit it by boat rental?

Caladesi Island State Park is one of Florida's finest natural beach destinations — 3 miles of undeveloped Gulf beach on an island with no bridge access. You can absolutely visit by boat rental from Chadillac Adventures at Clearwater Beach. The 20–30 minute ride north through Clearwater Harbor brings you to a pristine barrier island where you can anchor in the shallow Gulf water and walk an essentially untouched beach.

When is the best time for water sports at Clearwater Beach?

Clearwater Beach is excellent for water sports year-round, but October through May offers the best overall conditions — calm Gulf seas, pleasant temperatures (70–85°F), and excellent water clarity. Summer (June–August) is warm and active but afternoon thunderstorms are a daily feature and Gulf water can be rougher. March–April and October–November are peak season sweet spots with good weather and manageable crowds.
