FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I book a jet ski or boat rental?+

Click "Book Now" on any operator card. You'll be taken directly to their FareHarbor booking page where you can select date, time and number of riders. All bookings are confirmed instantly online.

Do I need a license to rent a jet ski in Florida?+

In Florida, anyone born after January 1, 1988 must have a boating safety ID card to operate a personal watercraft. Most rental operators offer a brief safety course on-site. Always confirm with the operator before booking.

What is the minimum age for jet ski rentals?+

Most operators require riders to be at least 18 years old to drive a jet ski. Passengers can typically be younger. Age requirements vary by operator — check their listing for specifics.

Are the operators on this site licensed and insured?+

Yes. All operators listed on MiamiJetskiBoatRentals.com are active FareHarbor partners operating legally in Florida. We recommend confirming insurance details directly with each operator before your activity.

Can I cancel or reschedule my booking?+

Cancellation and rescheduling policies vary by operator. Most allow free cancellation 24–48 hours before the activity. Review the specific cancellation policy on each operator's FareHarbor booking page before confirming.

What areas of Florida do you cover?+

We cover operators statewide — Miami Beach, Fort Lauderdale, Florida Keys, Gulf Coast (Naples, Fort Myers, Sarasota, St. Petersburg), Palm Beach, Space Coast, Everglades, Central Florida and Northeast FL.

What's the difference between a Slingshot and a Jet Car?+

A Polaris Slingshot is a 3-wheeled open-air autocycle you drive on roads. A Jet Car (also called a Jet Capsule or Water Car) is a vehicle that drives on water like a speedboat but looks like a sports car. Both are unique Florida experiences!
