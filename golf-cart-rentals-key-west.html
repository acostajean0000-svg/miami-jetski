🗺️ Best Routes to Explore by Golf Cart in Key West

Old Town & Duval Street Loop

Start at Mallory Square, cruise down Duval Street (the main drag), turn through the quiet residential streets of the Historic District with their Victorian homes and bougainvillea-draped fences. Stop at the Southernmost Point buoy — mandatory photo stop. The whole loop takes about 45 minutes with stops.

White Street to Smathers Beach

Head east from Old Town along White Street and Atlantic Boulevard to Smathers Beach, Key West's longest public beach. Stop at the various food trucks along the way. A peaceful contrast to the busy Duval Street scene.

Stock Island & The Working Waterfront

Cross the bridge to Stock Island — Key West's less-touristy neighbor. Home to working fishing docks, local restaurants, and the Key West Botanical Garden. Most visitors never make it here, which makes it all the more worth exploring.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can you drive a golf cart on Key West streets?

Yes. Street-legal Low Speed Vehicles (LSVs) are permitted on all Key West roads with speed limits of 35 mph or less — which covers most of the island. You cannot take them on US-1 (Overseas Highway).

Are golf carts available to pick up at the cruise terminal?

Several rental shops are within walking distance of the cruise pier on Mallory Square. Cruise ship passengers can rent a golf cart for the day and easily cover the whole island before departure.

How many people fit in a golf cart in Key West?

Standard carts seat 4 people. Extended/stretch carts seat 6. Confirm when booking if you need the larger size.
