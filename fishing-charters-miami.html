🐟 What to Catch in Miami

Inshore (Biscayne Bay)

Snook, redfish, tarpon, permit, and bonefish thrive in the shallow flats of Biscayne Bay. Fly fishing and light tackle are popular here. Best season: spring and fall.

Nearshore (Reefs & Wrecks)

The reefs off Miami are loaded with snapper, grouper, amberjack, and cobia. Half-day trips targeting these species are great for families and beginners.

Offshore (Gulf Stream)

Miami is one of the closest ports to the Gulf Stream in the US — just 3–5 miles out. This brings billfish, mahi-mahi, tuna, and wahoo within easy reach. The best offshore season runs from April through July.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a fishing license in Miami?

No. When fishing aboard a licensed charter, the captain's license covers all passengers. You do not need your own fishing license.

What is the best time to go fishing in Miami?

Year-round, but April through July is peak offshore season. Inshore fishing is great October through May. Sailfish are most abundant December through February.

What should I bring on a fishing charter?

Sunscreen, polarized sunglasses, non-slip shoes, snacks, drinks, and a hat. The charter provides all rods, bait, and tackle. Bring a cooler if you plan to keep your catch.
