🌿 Types of Everglades Experiences

Airboat Rides (Most Popular)

A flat-bottomed boat powered by a massive fan that skims across the surface of the "River of Grass" at up to 45 mph. You'll glide through sawgrass prairies, cypress swamps, and mangrove channels. Guides point out alligators, herons, anhingas, and other wildlife along the way. Ear protection is provided — it's loud.

Swamp Buggy Safari

A unique Everglades experience on a large, high-clearance all-terrain vehicle that can drive through shallow water and thick vegetation. Closer to the ground than an airboat, with a different perspective on the ecosystem. Captain Steve's Swamp Buggy is one of the most well-known operators in the region.

Guided Kayak / Canoe Tour

The quietest and most intimate way to experience the Everglades. Paddle through narrow mangrove channels where wildlife comes to you — no engine noise to scare animals away. Best for nature lovers and photographers.

Everglades National Park Day Trip

Full-day tours that combine an airboat ride with a visit to Everglades National Park's main visitor facilities, including the Anhinga Trail (the best short wildlife walk in Florida) and Flamingo Point at the southernmost tip of the Florida peninsula.

🦅 What Wildlife Will You See?

The Everglades are home to an astonishing variety of wildlife. On a typical tour you can expect to see American alligators (year-round, guaranteed), great blue herons, snowy egrets, anhingas, roseate spoonbills, and cormorants. With luck you might spot a Florida manatee, a bald eagle, or even an endangered Florida panther near dusk.

📅 Best Time to Visit the Everglades from Miami

November through April is the dry season — lower water levels concentrate wildlife near water sources, making sightings more frequent. Temperatures are comfortable (65–80°F). May through October is wet season — greener, with more birds nesting, but also hotter, more humid, and buggy. Bring insect repellent year-round.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How far is the Everglades from Miami?

The main entrance areas are 30–45 minutes southwest of downtown Miami by car. Tour operators with hotel pickup typically add 20–30 minutes for collection.

Is it safe to visit the Everglades?

Yes, on guided tours. Licensed operators know the environment well and keep guests safe from wildlife. Alligators are wild animals — stay on marked paths and respect distance guidelines.

What should I wear to the Everglades?

Light, breathable clothing in neutral colors. Closed-toe shoes (sandals are not recommended). A hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, and insect repellent are essential year-round.
